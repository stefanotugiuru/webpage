Pipi e Patati

Dec 17, 2025 5 min read Recipes
by Stefano Maurici

Watch the recipe video

Some dishes tell the story of a place better than words ever could. Pipi and Potatoes is one of them: a simple, rustic recipe straight from the heart of Calabria.

This dish is all about balance. Spicy peppers, golden potatoes and good olive oil come together to create something honest and deeply comforting. No shortcuts, no fancy techniques — just real ingredients and time.

Ingredients

  • Fresh peppers (preferably spicy)
  • Medium potatoes
  • Onion (optional)
  • Extra virgin olive oil
  • Salt, to taste
  • Black pepper, to taste
  • Chili pepper
  • (Optional) Fresh basil

Method

  1. Wash the peppers, remove the ends and slice them into rings or strips. Keep the seeds if you like it extra spicy, or remove them for a milder flavor.
  2. Peel the potatoes and cut them into thin slices or small cubes. You can rinse them to remove excess starch if you prefer.
  3. Heat a generous amount of extra virgin olive oil in a large pan. Add the potatoes and cook over medium heat for about 10 minutes, until they start to turn golden.
  4. Add the peppers to the pan, mix well and cover with a lid.
  5. Cook over medium-low heat, stirring occasionally, until the potatoes are soft and nicely browned. This will take around 20 minutes.
  6. If you like, add the sliced onion towards the end of the cooking time.
  7. Season with salt, black pepper and chili to taste.
  8. Serve hot, optionally finished with a few fresh basil leaves.

A personal tip

This recipe works best when you don’t rush it. Low heat, a wide pan and a bit of patience make all the difference. Perfect as a side dish, but also great as a rustic appetizer to share.
