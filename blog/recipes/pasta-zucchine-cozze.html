Pasta with Zucchini and Mussels

Jan 2026 6 min read Recipes
by Stefano Maurici

Watch the recipe video

Pasta with zucchini and mussels is a refined take on a classic seafood combination. The recipe is built around clarity and restraint, allowing the natural sweetness of the mussels to remain central.

Aromatics are layered carefully and the wine is allowed to evaporate fully, creating a balanced sauce that supports rather than overwhelms.

Ingredients (for 4 people)

  • 350 g pasta (linguine or spaghetti)
  • 1–1.5 kg fresh mussels
  • 3 zucchini
  • 1 chili pepper
  • 5 garlic cloves
  • Parsley stems (from 1 bunch)
  • Fresh parsley leaves, chopped
  • 1 ripe tomato
  • Dry white wine
  • Extra virgin olive oil

Method

  1. Clean the mussels thoroughly, removing the beard and scrubbing the shells. Set aside.
  2. In a large pot heat olive oil with two crushed garlic cloves, parsley stems, tomato and a pinch of chili. Add the mussels, deglaze with white wine, cover and cook until opened. Shell the mussels and strain the cooking liquid.
  3. Slice the zucchini thinly. In a wide pan heat olive oil with the remaining garlic and chili. Add the zucchini and sauté until tender but still vibrant.
  4. Add the shelled mussels and some of their strained liquid to the zucchini. Let the sauce reduce slightly.
  5. Cook the pasta in lightly salted water. Drain al dente and transfer directly to the pan. Toss for 1–2 minutes, adding more mussel liquid if needed.
  6. Finish with fresh parsley and a drizzle of olive oil before serving.

A critical note

With garlic, chili, wine and tomato all present, balance is essential. Use the tomato only as a background note and let the wine evaporate fully. If the flavor becomes too aggressive, remove the garlic during the zucchini stage.
